1.0 Executive Overview

The interconnection of nanoscale devices with existing communication networks defines the Internet of Nano-Things (IoNT). Driven by the convergence of nanotechnology and IoT, this paradigm shifts focus from macro-sensing to granular, molecular-level data acquisition.

Nano-Sensors

Devices 10-100nm in size detecting chemical and biological stimuli. Uses graphene-based plasmonic antennas for THz band communication.

WBAN Integration

Wireless Body Area Networks monitor vital signs (ECG, EEG, Glucose) continuously, shifting healthcare from institutional to preventative.

Self-Powered

Batteryless operation via microscale energy scavenging (Piezoelectric, Thermoelectric, RF) is critical for implant longevity.

2.0 Network Architecture

HIERARCHY: DEFINED

The Nanoscale Hierarchy

The IoNT architecture requires a hierarchical approach to bridge the gap between nano-communication (THz band) and the macro-world (Internet).

01

Nano-Nodes

Simplest devices. Limited energy/memory. Perform simple sensing and actuation. Transmission range: Short.

02

Nano-Routers

Aggregators with higher computational resources. They control nano-nodes and aggregate data for uplink.

03

Nano-Micro Interface

Hybrid devices communicating in both nanoscale (THz) and microscale (classic RF). The bridge to the gateway.

3.0 Energy Harvesting Matrices

Microscale Scavenging

Batteries are invasive and finite. The IoNT paradigm relies on harvesting ambient energy. Hybrid systems (TEG + PZT + RF) offer the highest reliability.

Photovoltaic (Outdoor) 100 mW/cm²
Thermoelectric (Human Body ΔT) 60 µW/cm²
Piezoelectric (Vibration) 200 µW/cm³
RF Scavenging (GSM/WiFi) 1 µW/cm²

Piezoelectric Mechanism

Based on charge separation. Mechanical strain in piezoelectric material creates potential difference. No separate voltage source required.

4.0 LaGOON Routing Protocol

Last Good Neighbor (LaGOON)

A simple, energy-efficient routing protocol designed for Wireless Nanosensor Networks (WNSNs). Unlike complex TCP/IP stacks, LaGOON minimizes overhead by exploiting the duplex nature of wireless transmission.

Mechanism: Backward Learning

Nodes update their routing tables based on incoming packets. If a packet arrives from 'Node A' with a lower hop count, 'Node A' is marked as the "Last Good Neighbor" for the return path.

5.0 LCPC Error Correction

Low-Complexity Parity Check Codes for WBAN Reliability

The Problem: LDPC Complexity

Standard LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check) codes require large matrices and iterative decoding (10-50 iterations), consuming too much power for nanosensors.

> LDPC Latency: High
> Memory Req: Large
> Iterations: >20

The Solution: LCPC Codes

LCPC extends Hamming codes to correct single and double-bit errors without iteration. Uses a simple lookup table approach.

> LCPC Latency: Low
> Memory Req: ~501 bits
> Iterations: 0

Performance Gain: LCPC (9,4) provides a 3 dB coding gain over LDPC at BER 10⁻⁵ with significantly reduced computational load.
